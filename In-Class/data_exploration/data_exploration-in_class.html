Analysis
To see the relationship of continuous variables such as GDP and Inflation Rate to the categorical target it might be useful to bin them for visual purposes. Based on the Histograms it seems that roughly three bins for both variables seem to be good. These bins could be interpreted as "Low", "Moderate", and "High" Inflation Rate or GDP respectively.

It can be seen from the count plots that a lots of students tend to drop when Inflation rate is very low or very high but not as much when it is moderate. Interestingly more students tend to drop during moderate and high GDP compared to lower GDP, these features could perhaps be looked at later in a cluster analysis to identify some pattern.

Most students tend to graduate or stay enrolled when inflation is very low and GDP is very high.

Analysis

  • Gender: Gender does not seem to play that much of a role in dropouts, however there is a higher proportion of graduates that are women than men in this dataset.
  • Educational Special Needs: educational special needs seems to have a negligible effect on dropout, graduate, or enrolled rates
  • Target: a vast majority of students in the dataset do graduate or stay enrolled, with a significant portion dropping out
  • Scholarship Holder: The data shows that students who hold scholarships drop out from course less than students who do not, this implies that holding scholarships means a student is less likely to drop.
  • International: The dataset does not have that many international students so it cannot be said how this affects the drop proportions.
  • Displaced: Students who are displaced seem to have a similar quantity of dropping out than those who are not, though it is slightly less than students who have not been displaced.
